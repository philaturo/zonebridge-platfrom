# Problem Statement

> **Version:** 1.0  
> **Status:** Draft

---

## Overview

Zone01 Kisumu is built around project-based learning, peer collaboration, and continuous knowledge sharing. Apprentices learn by solving increasingly complex technical challenges together, reviewing one another's work, and contributing to a community where learning extends beyond the classroom.

While the existing learning platform provides an excellent environment for delivering curriculum, project submissions, evaluations, and progress tracking, collaboration often extends across multiple independent tools and informal communication channels.

As the community grows, these interactions become increasingly difficult to discover, coordinate, and preserve.

---

## The Challenge

Every project completed within the Zone01 ecosystem generates valuable knowledge.

Architectural decisions, implementation techniques, debugging experiences, audit feedback, and lessons learned all contribute to the collective experience of the community.

However, much of this knowledge remains isolated within individual repositories, temporary conversations, or personal notes.

As a result, future apprentices frequently solve problems that have already been solved by previous members of the community.

---

## Identified Gaps

### Knowledge Fragmentation

Technical knowledge produced during projects is often scattered across repositories, messaging platforms, documentation, and personal discussions.

Without a shared knowledge space, valuable implementation experience becomes difficult to discover and is frequently lost over time.

---

### Expertise Discovery

Every apprentice develops strengths in different technologies throughout the curriculum.

Despite this, discovering who has experience with a particular language, framework, or project often depends on personal familiarity rather than a structured system.

This makes peer learning less accessible, particularly for new apprentices.

---

### Audit Coordination

Audits are an essential part of the Zone01 learning experience.

While the official platform assigns audit responsibilities, communication between auditors and apprentices frequently occurs outside the learning platform.

This introduces unnecessary coordination overhead and makes it difficult to communicate availability or request timely evaluations.

---

### Community Collaboration

Technical discussions, bootcamps, workshops, hackathons, and open-source initiatives are often organized across multiple independent platforms.

Without a central collaborative space, community activities become fragmented and difficult to discover.

---

### Knowledge Continuity

Each graduating cohort accumulates significant practical experience throughout the curriculum.

Without mechanisms for preserving and organizing that experience, future apprentices are unable to benefit from previous technical insights beyond publicly available repositories.

---

## Existing Ecosystem

Zone01 Kisumu already provides excellent tools for learning, project evaluation, and curriculum delivery.

Similarly, platforms such as Gitea and community messaging tools effectively address source control and communication.

ZoneBridge does not seek to replace these systems.

Instead, it focuses on strengthening collaboration between them by providing a dedicated platform where community knowledge, mentorship, audits, technical communities, and collaborative initiatives can coexist.

---

## Opportunity

A dedicated collaborative platform creates opportunities to strengthen peer learning without disrupting existing workflows.

By improving discoverability, preserving technical knowledge, and supporting community-driven initiatives, the platform can reduce unnecessary collaboration friction while encouraging greater participation across the Zone01 ecosystem.

The result is a community where knowledge is easier to find, collaboration is easier to initiate, and contributions continue benefiting apprentices long after individual projects have been completed.

---

## Success Criteria

The problem addressed by ZoneBridge can be considered successfully reduced when:

- Technical knowledge remains accessible across cohorts.
- Apprentices can easily discover peers with relevant expertise.
- Audit coordination becomes more transparent and efficient.
- Community initiatives are easier to organize and participate in.
- Open-source collaboration becomes a natural extension of the learning experience.
- Valuable implementation experience continues benefiting future members of the community.
