# Community

> **Version:** 1.0
> **Status:** Draft

---

## Overview

The ZoneBridge community is composed of apprentices, mentors, maintainers, contributors, and organizers who collaborate to strengthen the Zone01 Kisumu learning experience.

The platform exists to support these interactions by making collaboration easier, knowledge more accessible, and community initiatives easier to organize.

Rather than defining users solely by the actions they perform within the software, ZoneBridge recognizes the different ways individuals contribute to the growth of the community.

---

## Community Philosophy

The ZoneBridge community is founded on peer learning.

Every member has the opportunity to learn, contribute, teach, receive support, and participate in the continuous improvement of the community.

Knowledge is treated as a shared resource rather than an individual asset.

The platform encourages participation over competition and collaboration over isolation.

---

## Community Members

Every authenticated member of the Zone01 Kisumu community becomes part of the ZoneBridge community.

Community members may participate in one or more collaborative spaces depending on their interests and activities.

Participation naturally evolves over time as apprentices gain experience and contribute in different ways.

---

## Apprentices

Apprentices form the primary audience of the platform.

They participate by:

- joining communities
- requesting audits
- contributing knowledge
- asking for help
- mentoring peers
- participating in events
- collaborating on open-source initiatives

Every apprentice is both a learner and a potential contributor.

---

## Mentors

Mentors share practical experience and guide other community members.

Mentorship within ZoneBridge is collaborative rather than hierarchical.

Experienced apprentices are encouraged to support others through discussions, technical guidance, and shared implementation experience.

---

## Contributors

Contributors improve the ZoneBridge platform itself.

Contributions may include:

- software development
- documentation
- design
- testing
- bug reports
- feature proposals
- community improvements

ZoneBridge is developed openly, and contributions from the community are encouraged.

---

## Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for the long-term health of the platform.

Their responsibilities include:

- reviewing contributions
- maintaining engineering quality
- evolving the architecture
- ensuring documentation remains accurate
- guiding technical direction

Maintainers act as stewards of the project rather than gatekeepers.

---

## Organizers

Organizers coordinate collaborative initiatives within the community.

Examples include:

- bootcamps
- hackathons
- workshops
- study groups
- technical communities
- community events

ZoneBridge provides tools that help organizers communicate, coordinate, and engage participants.

---

## Participation

Community participation extends beyond writing code.

Members contribute by:

- sharing knowledge
- reviewing work
- mentoring peers
- organizing events
- improving documentation
- reporting issues
- participating in discussions
- contributing to open-source projects

Every meaningful contribution strengthens the platform and the community.

---

## Community Values

The ZoneBridge community values:

- Respect
- Collaboration
- Curiosity
- Openness
- Continuous Learning
- Technical Excellence
- Shared Responsibility

These values guide interactions across the platform.
